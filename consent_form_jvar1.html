HIT name: How variable is this group of items?

This is a consent form. You can start the HIT by pressing the Agree and Continue button below on this page.

Task instructions and practice trials will be provided on the next page.

1. Purpose

This is a study about estimating the variability of items having various colors and orientations.

2. Duration and Procedure

130 Americans who have a normal or corrected-to-normal vision of 0.8 or higher within 6 months and normal color vision, speaking English as a native language, and with age 18 and over will be recruited.

In this study, you will be asked to watch a group of items having various orientations and colors, and to judge how variable this group of items. The HIT takes about 20 minutes.

Before starting the main trials, there will be a familiarization phase which will take about a minute. During this period, several example items will be presented sequentially. There will be no task that required to do, and you can just watch example items. The purpose of the familiarization phase is to provide you an overall sense of the items that will be presented in the main trials.

After the familiarization phase, the main trials will begin with a few practice trials. In each trial, you will be asked to watch a group of items having various orientations and colors. You will be asked to judge how variable this group of items. You can judge that items with similar colors and orientations are less variable, and items with dissimilar colors and orientations are more variable. To report the variability of items, you can adjust the slider bar shown below the items. Please adjust the bar on the slider toward right if you feel that the variability of items appears large, and adjust it toward left if you feel that their variability appears low. There is no time limit to report. If you finish the adjustment of the slider bar, click the continue button below the slider to proceed to the next trials. During the HIT, you may feel that your eyes are tired because of attending to displays. In this case, you can rest as much as you want before moving onto the next trial by not starting the next trial.

3. Risks and Benefits

  • Risks: There are minimal to no risks to your safety posed by this study. If you ever feel uncomfortable during the experiment, you may stop at any time.
  • Compensations: You will receive $3 compensation for participation.
  • Benefits: There are no direct benefits for participating in this study. However, the information you provide will help us better understand visual perception.

4. Privacy Protection

This research does not collect any private information. Demographic information such as age or gender are collected, but this information will not be identified with any specific individual. Collected data will be used for research purposes only, and participants' responses will be kept confidential.

5. Compensation for Injuries

This study has minimal to no psychological risks or side effects. For further inquiries regarding this research, we provide you the contact information of researchers.

6. Personal Information and Confidentiality

Collected data will be preserved as computerized data files. For promoting open science, the data can be deposited in a public storage place like OSF (Open Science Framework), but the deposited data will not have any personal information. Any academic presentation of publication regarding the data collected herein will not include any information that can be connected with individual participants.

7. Freedom to Withdraw or Refuse Participation

You have the right to stop participating in a research, for any reason, and at any time. If you withdraw from participation, we will neither keep your data nor include it in our statistical analysis. Based on the policies of the Amazon Mechanical Turk, the monetary reward will not be provided for those who withdraw from the study.
